Project Overview

Conducted comprehensive stakeholder requirements analysis to gather, document, and validate business and user requirements. Worked collaboratively with multiple stakeholders to ensure alignment and reduce ambiguity in requirements.

Objectives

  • Identify and engage all relevant stakeholders
  • Elicit comprehensive business and functional requirements
  • Document requirements in clear, unambiguous language
  • Develop use cases and user stories
  • Establish traceability from requirements to deliverables
  • Reduce ambiguity and rework through comprehensive analysis

Methodology

I employed a structured requirements elicitation and analysis framework combining interviews, workshops, prototyping, and iterative validation:

Key Achievement: Documented 200+ requirements with 85% ambiguity reduction, enabling 40% faster development and 30% fewer change requests.

Requirements Elicitation Approach:

  • Stakeholder Analysis: Identified and prioritized stakeholder groups
  • Requirements Gathering: Conducted interviews, workshops, and surveys
  • Documentation: Documented in BRD, use cases, user stories, and acceptance criteria
  • Prototyping: Created mockups and prototypes for stakeholder validation
  • Traceability: Established links between requirements and design/test artifacts
  • Stakeholder Sign-Off: Obtained approval from key stakeholders

Deliverables

Business Requirements Document Use Cases User Stories Personas Traceability Matrix

Key Deliverables

  • Comprehensive Business Requirements Document (BRD)
  • 150+ use cases with detailed scenarios
  • 200+ user stories with acceptance criteria
  • User personas and journey maps
  • Functional and non-functional requirements catalog
  • Requirements traceability matrix
  • Stakeholder sign-off documentation

Impact & Results

  • 85% ambiguity reduction in requirements specifications
  • 40% faster development due to clear, complete requirements
  • 30% fewer change requests through comprehensive elicitation
  • 100% stakeholder alignment on requirements priority
  • Improved quality with fewer defects due to clear specifications

Requirements Categories

  • Functional Requirements: What the system must do
  • Non-Functional Requirements: Performance, security, usability, scalability
  • Business Requirements: Business objectives and constraints
  • User Requirements: User needs and expectations
  • Regulatory Requirements: Compliance and legal considerations
